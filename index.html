I have 3+ years of experience in assisting research and providing technology support for remote learning. I also take an active interest in community service and have convinced a former employer to set up library van projects for two rural communities as part of its social responsibility strategy, and which I successfully managed until 2014. Besides an MLIS, I hold a Master of Science Degree in Forestry from the University of British Columbia where I worked with Dr. Terry Sunderland at the Sunderland Lab on a dissertation that explored solutions for disseminating conservation information in high forest zone natural resource-dependent communities. Research-wise, I am interested in exploring trends and issues in data management for scientific publication. I am also curious about understanding user experiences of virtual reference services within academic and research libraries. Recently however, my interest has focused more on the quantitative aspects of information and the quantitative features and characteristics of scientific literature. I've used bibliometric, machine learning, and text mining and analytic techniques to explore social, geographic, and institutional dynamics in scientific knowledge production and exchange.
