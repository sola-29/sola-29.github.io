I have 3+ years of experience in assisting research and providing technology support for remote learning. I also take an active interest in community service and have helped set up library van projects for two rural communities as a social responsibility strategy of a former employer. Beside MLIS, I hold a Master of Science Degree in Forestry from the University of British Columbia where I worked with Dr. Terry Sunderland at the Sunderland Lab on a dissertation that explored solutions for disseminating conservation information in high forest zone natural resource-dependent communities. Research-wise, I am interested in the quantitative aspects of information and the quantitative features and characteristics of scientific literature. I use bibliometric, machine learning, and text mining and analytic techniques to explore social, geographic, and institutional dynamics in scientific knowledge production and exchange.
